## Overview: Why Annual Checkups Matter

Regular health checkups establish baseline health information and identify potential problems before they become serious. These preventive visits allow early intervention and ongoing health maintenance <CitationRef index={1} />.

Annual checkups provide opportunity to discuss health concerns, review medications, and ensure all preventive services are current.

<InfoBox title="Key Information">
A complete checkup includes vital signs assessment, physical examination, health history review, and selected screening tests based on age and risk factors.
</InfoBox>

## Vital Signs and Physical Exam

Blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, respiratory rate, and weight are measured. The physician performs thorough examination including heart, lungs, abdomen, and lymph nodes. Vision and hearing are assessed.

## Screening Tests

Laboratory work may include blood tests evaluating cholesterol, glucose, liver and kidney function, and CBC. Depending on age and risk, additional tests screen for cancer, osteoporosis, or other conditions.

<WarningBox>
Prepare a list of current medications, supplements, and herbal products. Bring insurance cards and identification. List any symptoms or health concerns to discuss.
</WarningBox>

<ClinicalNote>
Schedule checkups annually, or more frequently if you have chronic conditions <CitationRef index={2} />. Come prepared with questions and honest answers about lifestyle, family history, and symptoms. Use this time to optimize your health proactively.
</ClinicalNote>

## After Your Checkup

Request results summary and follow-up recommendations. Schedule any necessary specialist referrals. Plan lifestyle modifications and medication adjustments as recommended by your provider.
