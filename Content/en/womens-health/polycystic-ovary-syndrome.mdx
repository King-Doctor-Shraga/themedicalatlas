## Overview: What Is PCOS?

Polycystic ovary syndrome is a complex endocrine disorder characterized by hormonal imbalance and metabolic dysfunction. It's the most common endocrine disorder in reproductive-age women, affecting fertility, metabolism, and long-term health <CitationRef index={1} />.

PCOS presents with irregular periods, elevated androgen levels (male hormones), and ovulatory dysfunction. While multiple small follicles may appear on ovaries, the name reflects an outdated understanding; many women without cysts have PCOS, and many with ovarian cysts don't have the syndrome.

<InfoBox title="Key Information">
PCOS diagnosis requires two of three criteria: irregular ovulation, clinical or biochemical hyperandrogenism, and polycystic ovary appearance on ultrasound. Hyperinsulinemia and insulin resistance are common features.
</InfoBox>

## Symptoms and Signs

Irregular or absent periods are hallmark symptoms. Women often experience excessive hair growth (hirsutism), acne, and male-pattern baldness. Many struggle with weight gain, particularly abdominal weight distribution. Some experience infertility or difficulty conceiving.

## Diagnosis and Evaluation

Diagnosis involves blood tests measuring testosterone, DHEA-S, and LH/FSH ratios. Pelvic ultrasound visualizes ovarian structure. Metabolic assessment includes fasting glucose, insulin levels, and glucose tolerance testing.

<WarningBox>
PCOS increases risks for type 2 diabetes (5 times), metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disease, and endometrial cancer. Regular screening and preventive care are essential.
</WarningBox>

<ClinicalNote>
Treatment emphasizes lifestyle modifications: weight loss of 5-10% improves ovulation and metabolic parameters <CitationRef index={2} />. Medications address specific concerns: oral contraceptives regulate cycles, metformin improves insulin sensitivity, and anti-androgens reduce excessive hair growth. Fertility treatment options exist for those desiring pregnancy.
</ClinicalNote>

## Managing PCOS Long-Term

Maintenance requires ongoing monitoring, regular exercise, balanced nutrition focusing on lower glycemic index foods, and stress management. Annual screening for diabetes and cardiovascular disease is important.
