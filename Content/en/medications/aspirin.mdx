## What Is Aspirin?

Aspirin is a nsaid/antiplatelet medication used primarily to treat pain, inflammation, cardiovascular protection. This drug has been widely used in clinical practice for several decades and remains an important treatment option in modern medicine. <CitationRef index={1} /> demonstrates the clinical effectiveness of this medication in managing a variety of conditions.

The medication works by affecting specific physiological processes in the body, making it valuable for both acute and chronic treatment scenarios. Healthcare providers frequently prescribe Aspirin due to its well-established safety profile and efficacy data. <CitationRef index={1} /> provides comprehensive evidence supporting its therapeutic use.

<InfoBox title="How Aspirin Works">
Aspirin operates through specific biochemical mechanisms that target the underlying cause of the condition being treated. The drug interacts with cellular receptors and enzymes to produce its therapeutic effects, leading to symptom relief and improved patient outcomes.
</InfoBox>

## Indications

Aspirin is prescribed for:
- Pain, inflammation, cardiovascular protection
- Prevention of related complications
- Symptom management in chronic conditions
- Acute symptom relief in appropriate patients

## Dosage and Administration

**Typical dosing:** 81-325 mg daily

**Maximum daily dose:** 4000 mg daily

Dosing may be adjusted based on individual patient factors including age, renal function, hepatic function, and concurrent medications. Always follow your healthcare provider's specific dosing instructions.

<ClinicalNote>
Individual dose adjustments may be necessary. Regular monitoring ensures optimal therapeutic effect while minimizing adverse effects. Consult with your healthcare provider before making any dose changes.
</ClinicalNote>

## Side Effects

**Common side effects:**
- Mild headache
- Gastrointestinal discomfort
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Fatigue

**Serious but rare side effects:**
- Allergic reactions
- Severe hepatotoxicity
- Hematologic abnormalities
- Severe dermatologic reactions

<WarningBox>
Seek immediate medical attention if you experience signs of allergic reaction, severe abdominal pain, unusual bleeding, or difficulty breathing.
</WarningBox>

## Contraindications

Aspirin should not be used in patients with:
- Known hypersensitivity to the drug
- Severe hepatic impairment
- Specific renal conditions (as appropriate)
- Pregnancy (in certain trimesters, as applicable)

## Drug Interactions

Aspirin may interact with various medications including:
- Other medications in the same class
- CYP450 enzyme inhibitors/inducers
- Anticoagulants
- NSAIDs

Always inform your healthcare provider about all medications you're taking.
