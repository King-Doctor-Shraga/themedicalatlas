## What Is Sinusitis?

Sinusitis, also called rhinosinusitis, is inflammation of the paranasal sinuses (air-filled cavities around the nose and eyes). <CitationRef index={1} /> The sinuses normally produce mucus that drains through the nose. When inflammation or infection blocks drainage, mucus accumulates, causing pain, congestion, and pressure. Sinusitis can be acute (lasting less than 4 weeks), subacute, or chronic (lasting more than 12 weeks).

Viral infections are the most common cause of acute sinusitis, while allergies and anatomical obstruction are common chronic causes.

<InfoBox title="Key Info">
Most acute sinusitis resolves without antibiotics. Treatment focuses on symptom relief and promoting drainage. Only bacterial infections require antibiotics.
</InfoBox>

## Causes and Risk Factors

Acute causes:
- Viral upper respiratory infection (most common)
- Bacterial infection
- Allergic rhinitis
- Irritant exposure

Chronic causes:
- Recurrent infections
- Allergies
- Anatomical obstruction (deviated septum, polyps)
- Immunosuppression
- Gastroesophageal reflux
- Cystic fibrosis

## Symptoms

Symptoms include:
- Nasal congestion
- Facial pain or pressure
- Nasal drainage (clear, yellow, or green)
- Cough (especially at night)
- Headache
- Fever (in acute bacterial infection)
- Hyposmia (reduced smell)
- Post-nasal drip

## Diagnosis

Diagnosis involves:
- Clinical history and symptoms
- Nasal examination (rhinoscopy)
- CT imaging (for chronic or complicated cases)
- Nasal endoscopy

<ClinicalNote>
Most acute viral sinusitis resolves spontaneously. Imaging is not recommended for uncomplicated acute sinusitis. <CitationRef index={2} />
</ClinicalNote>

## Treatment and Management

Acute viral sinusitis:
- Supportive care (fluids, rest)
- Nasal saline irrigation
- Decongestants (phenylephrine)
- Analgesics for pain
- Topical or systemic corticosteroids

Bacterial sinusitis:
- Broad-spectrum antibiotics (amoxicillin-clavulanate)
- Duration: 7-10 days

Chronic sinusitis:
- Nasal corticosteroid sprays
- Nasal saline irrigation
- Antihistamines (if allergic)
- Functional endoscopic sinus surgery (FESS)

## Prevention

Prevention strategies include:
- Hand hygiene
- Avoiding cigarette smoke
- Managing allergies
- Treating GERD
- Maintaining humidified environment
- Using saline irrigation regularly

<WarningBox>
Complications of sinusitis include orbital cellulitis, meningitis, and cavernous sinus thrombosis. Seek urgent care for fever, severe headache, vision changes, or eye swelling.
</WarningBox>

## When to See a Doctor

See a doctor if symptoms persist beyond 10 days, worsen after improvement, or are severe. Frequent recurrent sinusitis warrants specialist evaluation for underlying causes.
